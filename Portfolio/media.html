GIMP Layers

GIMP..

Thanks to GIMP, or the GNU Image Manipulation Program, it’s never been easier and more affordable to create epic looking thumbnails and high-quality logos. GIMP is a free to use, open-source answer to image editors looking for an Adobe Photoshop alternative. It’s also quite beginner friendly so you won't be lost while using it.... It's also filled with tips and tricks to help produce the alterations and revisions that your image requires.
On my first video to the left you will see a recording I made using GIMP. In there I demontrate how to get a layer and add it to blank page. It's simple, easy to use and practical. All you need is time to get the hang of it and inspire yourself when editing.


Audacity..

Audacity is the default software for many multimedia content creators because not only is it free, it’s also relatively easy to use. As a bonus, it doesn’t consume a lot of CPU power, so it can run on pretty much any computer.
When you open the software, you’ll start with a new file, and you can import an audio file or hit record. ... To import, head to [File > Import] and then find and click on the audio file you want. If you want to layer your audio with music, or if you have multiple files, you’ll want to have a track for each one, which makes it easier to edit.
Getting rid of noises and hummings is my reason for using Audacity! The low humming noise is annoying, and it ruins the listener’s or viewers’ experience, so to get rid of it you will need to; click and highlight a few seconds of noise within the clip (make sure it’s just noise) then click on [Effect > Noise Reduction], which you can find in the top menu. Select “Get Noise Profile,” which will close the “Noise Reduction” window, and then select the whole track, or whichever part of the track is noisy, and use [Effect > Noise Reduction] again. The “Noise Reduction” effect can get rid of noise coming from fans, A/C units, breathing, and other annoyances.


Camtasia..

Camtasia can be very easy once you get the hang of it! It all begins with a planning and from then onwards, it's simple steps to follow.
Step 1. Write a Script! And don't forget to use words you’d use in everyday conversations. This will give your script a smooth, natural feel. Once you have the script ready, it's all about practice.... Step 2. Record the audio narration! Start by getting your hands on the best microphone you can find. This will make a huuuuge difference (trust me on that). Usually that's where Audacity comes in; but you can also use Camtasia's recorder if you just want something simple.
Step 3. Record video of your screen! Make sure to clean up your desktop, and close the other applications.
Step 4. Cut out mistakes and trim extra footage! This is usually the boring part as you may have to watch the video a thousand times.. But it's important to clean up the mistakes we usually make. After that, once you're happy, you just have to upload an share your video to others.
